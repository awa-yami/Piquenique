Welcome

Hello, welcome to my website. My name is Yamí and I'm 25 years old. I'm studying Applied Foreign Languages at UnB and grew up in Brasília. If you're the kind of person who likes to picnic like me, you're in the best place to discover good places to do so in the Cruzeiro neighborhood and in the Sudoeste/Octogonal neighborhood area. What's more, you'll see some great places to visit near the picnic sites.

The locations have been selected because they arouse some affection in the creator of this site.

Picnic Spots

Dona Sarah Kubitschek City Park

The first place I would recommend is the City Park -Parque da Cidade-, the largest urban park in Latin America with approximately 420 hectares designed by landscape architect Burle Marx incorporating elements of the Cerrado biome, using native species. Others responsible for the park's design were urban planner Lúcio Costa, architect Oscar Niemeyer and artist Athos Bulcão. The park was founded on October 11, 1978.

The City Park has a number of attractions for all ages, such as barbecue areas, sports courts, playgrounds, squares, lakes, an Equestrian Center, restaurants and an extensive Exhibition Pavilion. The venue receives an average of 14,000 people from Monday to Friday and 37,000 at weekends. During special events, the attendance rises to 80,000, according to the DF Secretariat for Sport and Leisure.

The park has many green areas, trees that provide shade and water mirrors that make the weather milder and more comfortable. The park also has 16 rest stops with toilets designed by Glauco Campello along its 10 km of running and cycling paths. The masonry walls of the rest stops are covered with a panel of tiles created by Athos Bulcão. The park is located between the Asa Sul neighborhood in Plano Piloto and the Sudoeste Sector. It's very busy and there are lots of food stalls.

I particularly enjoy going out with friends in this park, rollerblading, walking, chatting... and then we take a cane, a cloth, spread out on the grass and under a tree for shade, and have a snack together. It's very relaxing. That's leisure. But watch out for anthills when you leave your yoke on the grass. I'd also recommend taking care of the time of day, as there are mosquitoes from dusk onwards.

When I was a child, my family and I used to cycle along the park's cycle paths. We did this on holidays or at weekends. It was one of the main places to spend quality time together.

Sukata Skate Park

The Sukata Skate Park -Pista de Skate Sukata-, or Skate Park, as it is popularly known, is located between the Octogonal and Cruzeiro Novo neighborhoods. It has a skate park covering more than 1,500 m², and is the oldest skate park in the Federal District. There is also a community meeting point where residents can exercise on the equipment. There are also benches, trees and grass for picnics. The skate park has recently undergone a refurbishment and from time to time there are rock concerts and skate culture events.

The location is next to the shopping center Terraço Shopping, where there is the Lojas Americanas convenience store and the Daiso store and next to the Veneza supermarket, which can be good options if you want to buy a snack nearby quickly and reasonably cheaply.

During my childhood, as my brother and some of my friends nearby were skateboarders and would occasionally go to the skate park, I would end up going there too, even though I was never interested in skateboarding. A few years later, when exercise equipment was installed in the park near the skate park, I went back there with my mother to walk and exercise. It was well frequented in the early years, but then it lost its regulars.
